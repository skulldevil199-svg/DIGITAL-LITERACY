# Case Study: Cyberbullying – "The Silent Storm"

**Crime Type:** Cyberbullying  
**Scenario Type:** Realistic Fictional Scenario  

---

## What Is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of digital platforms — social media, messaging apps, online games, or email — to repeatedly harass, threaten, humiliate, or target an individual. Unlike traditional bullying, it follows the victim everywhere, 24 hours a day, with the added power of anonymity and a potentially massive audience.

---

## How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

1. **Triggering Event:** Riya, a first-year engineering student, posts a video of herself participating in a college cultural event on Instagram.

2. **Initial Attack:** A classmate, upset about a minor disagreement, begins sharing the video with mocking captions in a private WhatsApp group of 60+ students.

3. **Escalation:** Screenshots of Riya's personal photos are taken from her public profile and circulated with altered, offensive text. Anonymous accounts are created to post derogatory comments directly on her posts.

4. **Pile-On Effect:** Other students, some who do not even know Riya personally, join in — commenting negatively, creating memes, and flooding her DMs with hurtful messages.

5. **Isolation:** Riya begins to withdraw from class, stops posting online, and is afraid to attend college.

---

## Who Is Typically Targeted?

Students who are perceived as "different," newcomers to a social group, high academic achievers, or individuals who have publicly disagreed with a popular peer. First-year college students are especially vulnerable as they are still building social networks and may lack the confidence to respond.

---

## Consequences

- **Psychological:** Severe anxiety, depression, and in extreme cases, suicidal ideation.
- **Academic:** Drop in attendance and performance.
- **Social:** Long-term trust issues and reluctance to engage online or in person.
- **Legal:** In India, cyberbullying is punishable under IT Act Section 66A (as interpreted), Section 507 IPC (criminal intimidation by anonymous communication), and Section 354D IPC (stalking).
