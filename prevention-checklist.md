# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### Focus: Cyberbullying Awareness for College Students in India

---

## 🛡️ General Online Safety

1. **Set all social media profiles to Private** — Regularly audit who can see your posts, stories, and personal information. Limiting your audience reduces the risk of being targeted by strangers.

2. **Never share your passwords** — Do not share login credentials with friends, classmates, or anyone else. If your account is compromised, change your password immediately.

3. **Document and screenshot everything** — If you are being harassed online, take dated screenshots of all messages, posts, and profiles before reporting or blocking. These serve as evidence.

4. **Block and report bullies immediately** — Do not engage or respond to bullying behaviour. Use the platform's Block and Report features right away to stop the cycle.

5. **Talk to a trusted adult or counsellor** — Cyberbullying is not your fault. Speak to a parent, warden, professor, or the college counsellor as soon as possible.

---

## 💰 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory)

6. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — Legitimate banks, payment apps (PhonePe, GPay, Paytm), or college authorities will NEVER ask for your UPI PIN or OTP. Any request for this information is a scam.

7. **Verify the recipient's UPI ID before every transaction** — Double-check the name that appears on the confirmation screen before pressing "Pay." Fraudsters create IDs nearly identical to trusted contacts.

---

## 🌐 Identity & Privacy Protection

8. **Do not post your college ID, Aadhaar, or phone number publicly** — These details can be used to impersonate you, create fake profiles, or harass you offline as well as online.

---

## 📞 Reporting Channels (India)

| Resource | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) – Report cyberbullying, harassment, and fraud online |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** – 24x7 helpline for reporting cybercrime including cyberbullying and financial fraud |
| **College ICC / Anti-Ragging Cell** | Contact your institution's Internal Complaints Committee for campus-related cyberbullying |

---

> ⚠️ **Remember:** Cyberbullying is a crime in India. You have the right to report it. You are not alone.
