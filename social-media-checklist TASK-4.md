# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** — Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests.

2. **Think before you post** — Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?" If not, do not post it.

3. **Use privacy settings** — Regularly review and update the privacy settings on all your social media accounts to control who sees your content.

4. **Give credit where it is due** — Always acknowledge the original creator when sharing someone else's work, ideas, or images.

5. **Engage positively and constructively** — Comment and respond to others with respect, even when you disagree with their views.

6. **Report harmful content** — Use platform reporting tools to flag bullying, misinformation, or inappropriate content you come across.

7. **Separate personal and professional accounts** — Consider maintaining a professional LinkedIn profile separately from your casual social media accounts.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** — Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, Aadhaar details, or bank information on any public platform.

2. **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** — Do not bully others online, and do not stay silent if you witness someone being targeted. Report it immediately.

3. **Don't post impulsively when angry or upset** — Emotional posts made in the heat of the moment can damage your reputation permanently.

4. **Don't fall for fake news** — Always verify information from a credible source before sharing. Forwarding misinformation can have serious consequences.

5. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers** — Be cautious about who you allow into your digital circle, especially accounts with no mutual connections.

6. **Don't use social media to share exam papers or copyrighted material** — This is academically dishonest and can lead to disciplinary action.

7. **Don't tag others in embarrassing or compromising posts without their consent** — Always ask before tagging someone in content they may not be comfortable with.
